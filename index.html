F.A.Q.

How can I register for the course?
Apply to the course here. Applications will be ordered based on year, preparation, and future plans. If your application is accepted, Jeff will send you an override code.
I am a RISD student interested in taking the course. How do I enroll?
If you are sent an override code, there is a RISD form you will need Jeff to sign to register (bring it to the second day of class). Please refer to RISD cross-registration instructions.
Can I take the course as a capstone?
Yes, but you must extend an assignment into something that has substantial development and design components. This can be done as a group, but the amount of work should be proportional to the group size.
Will there be a textbook for the course?
No, all readings will be available on this website.
Will there be a final exam?
Nope! You will complete either a portfolio review or a group project for your final assignment.
Can I take this course as a non-CS concentrator?
The CS0130 track is designed for non-concentrators and has no prerequistes. However, CS1300 requires one of the intro CS sequences(CS15/16, CS17/18, or CS19) and some knowledge of Javascript, but you may still enroll in CS1300 if you satisfy those even as a non-CS concentrator.
